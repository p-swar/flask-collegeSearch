A Scholarship is a grant or payment made to support a student's education, awarded on the basis of academic or other achievement that you do not have to pay back
The New York State Tuition Assistance Program (TAP) helps eligible New York residents pay tuition at approved schools in NYC
The Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) is a form completed by current and prospective college students in the United States to determine their eligibility for student financial aid
A work study is a federally sponsered college program that enables students to work part-time while attending school
Tax credits help offset the costs (tuition, books, housing, exc) of college or career school by reducing the amount of your income tax
Educational Awards can be used to pay tuition at a Title IV institution of higher education, for educational training, or to repay your qualified student loans
Merit scholarships are typically awarded on the basis of academic, athletic or artistic merit, in addition to special interests
Net Price is the amount that a student pays to attend an institution in a single academic year AFTER subtracting scholarships and grants the student receives.
